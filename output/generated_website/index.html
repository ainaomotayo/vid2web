About the Film

This poignant stop-motion animated short, 'Birth, Life, Death,' delves into universal themes using intricate paper artistry. Produced by a student at the Rochester Institute of Technology's School of Film and Animation, the film unfolds through a series of evocative scenes: from the symbolic birth of an eye within a golden egg to the growth of a barren tree amidst a landscape of shredded words, and ultimately, the fiery dissolution of form, revealing an underlying structure. Each frame is a testament to meticulous craft, inviting viewers to reflect on creation, presence, and inevitable transformation.

Explore Key Scenes

Paper Cityscapes

Explore an atmospheric miniature world of buildings crafted from newspaper, illuminated by soft lamplight, and inhabited by a curious black cat silhouette.

The Eye of Existence

Witness the powerful metaphor of a golden egg cracking open to reveal a human eye, symbolizing awakening and perception amidst a world of narratives.

Literary Landscapes

Journey through a unique environment where a lone boat, holding a bare tree, rests upon a vast sea of shredded paper text, suggesting life's rootedness in stories and knowledge.

Fiery Transformation

Experience the intense sequence of a paper form consumed by flames, culminating in ash and the stark beauty of a skeletal wireframe, representing the cycle of destruction and emergence.
