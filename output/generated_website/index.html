About the Film

This captivating stop-motion animated film, 'Birth, Life, Death,' is a profound visual narrative that delves into the universal cycle of existence.

Utilizing innovative paper-based sculpting and intricate animation techniques, the film brings to life a world of fragility and transformation. Each scene is meticulously crafted, from a mysterious cityscape to symbolic natural landscapes, reflecting the delicate balance of creation and decay.

It is a thought-provoking exploration of life's transient beauty and ultimate metamorphosis. This student-produced work from the School of Film and Animation at Rochester Institute of Technology showcases a unique artistic vision.

The Artistry Behind the Frames

Intricate Stop-Motion Animation

Experience a unique visual style brought to life through precise frame-by-frame animation, showcasing fluid movement within static, handcrafted environments.

02

Paper Artistry & Sculpture

Witness a world constructed from paper, where every detail – from urban architecture to natural elements – is sculpted from books and discarded texts.

Symbolic Visual Storytelling

Follow a journey rich in symbolism, with elements like a hatching eye, a burgeoning tree, and a burning form representing profound stages of existence.

04

Universal Themes

Engage with a philosophical narrative that contemplates 'Birth, Life, Death' through allegorical imagery, inviting viewers to reflect on permanence.
